## Question
> The `2.5×` multiplier stacked on `VIDEO_CONFIG.BITRATE_MULTIPLIER` (`WorkerExporter:236`) deserves a dedicated paragraph in the troubleshooting doc — it's load-bearing for Chrome/Firefox bitrate-target parity but invisible in the UI.

## Answer

The encoder-bitrate expression is `config.bitrate * VIDEO_CONFIG.BITRATE_MULTIPLIER * 2.5` (`engine-gmt/engine/worker/WorkerExporter.ts:236`). The two factors do different jobs: `BITRATE_MULTIPLIER = 1_000_000` is a plain Mbps→bps unit conversion (`data/constants.ts:30`, comment "Mbps to bps"); the `2.5×` is a content-specific CBR-undershoot correction. The in-source comment block immediately above line 236 (`WorkerExporter.ts:230-235`) spells out the rationale: the slider value is a "visible bitrate" target, but CBR rate-control tends to undershoot the configured target on GMT's content — smooth gradients and low motion read as "easy" to the encoder — so the multiplier keeps player-reported bitrate close to what the user set.

The pairing is duplicated in the main-thread encoder path. `engine/export/videoEncoder.ts:114` uses the same `cfg.bitrate * VIDEO_CONFIG.BITRATE_MULTIPLIER * 2.5` expression and its preceding comment (`:107-109`) explicitly says "mirrors WorkerExporter". The `MainThreadEncoderConfig.bitrate` JSDoc at `videoEncoder.ts:27-28` re-states the contract ("Mbps; multiplied by `BITRATE_MULTIPLIER * 2.5` to compensate for CBR undershoot on smooth-gradient content"). Note that the codec-availability probe at `videoEncoder.ts:54` deliberately passes only `bitrateMbps * VIDEO_CONFIG.BITRATE_MULTIPLIER` (no `2.5×`) to `Mediabunny.canEncodeVideo` — capability testing uses the raw unit-converted value, only the live `encoder.configure(...)` call applies the inflation.

The browser-asymmetry consequence is called out at `WorkerExporter.ts:234-235`: on Firefox the `2.5×` inflation is moot because the OpenH264 Level-4.0 cap (~31 Mbps) bites first, regardless of what we request. That cap is documented in `docs/gmt/06_Troubleshooting_and_Quirks.md:48-50` ("Firefox H.264 export bitrate hard-capped at ~31 Mbps") with the user-facing mitigation: `RenderPopup.tsx`'s `isFirefoxH264BitrateCapped` shows an inline notice once the slider exceeds ~12 Mbps — that threshold is "≈ where the multiplied encoder request hits the OpenH264 ceiling" (`06_Troubleshooting_and_Quirks.md:50`), i.e. the 12 Mbps figure is itself derived from the `2.5×` multiplier (12 × 2.5 ≈ 30 Mbps, just under the ~31 Mbps OpenH264 cap).

So the multiplier is in fact partially documented today, but only as a side-clause inside the Firefox-cap quirk and inside the source comments at `WorkerExporter.ts:230-235` / `videoEncoder.ts:107-109` / `:27-28`. There is no standalone troubleshooting entry that names the `2.5×` factor, explains why a user-reported "I set 40 Mbps but the file is X" question requires accounting for both `BITRATE_MULTIPLIER` (unit) and the `2.5×` (CBR-undershoot compensation), and points readers at both encoder sites (worker + main-thread). The question's request is therefore valid: a dedicated paragraph in `06_Troubleshooting_and_Quirks.md` near the existing Firefox-cap entry would consolidate the three scattered comment blocks into one discoverable doc section. Confidence: high.

## Related findings

- Both encoder paths must be kept in sync — any future change to the `2.5×` factor (or its removal once Firefox ships a non-OpenH264 H.264 path) needs to land in both `engine-gmt/engine/worker/WorkerExporter.ts:236` and `engine/export/videoEncoder.ts:114` simultaneously, plus the JSDoc at `engine/export/videoEncoder.ts:27-28`.
- The capability probe asymmetry (`engine/export/videoEncoder.ts:54` omits the `2.5×`) is intentional but undocumented as such — worth a sentence in the same proposed paragraph so future readers don't "fix" the probe to match the configure call.
- The `RenderPopup.tsx` 12 Mbps threshold is the only UI surface of the multiplier today; if the `2.5×` factor changes, that threshold needs to move with it.
