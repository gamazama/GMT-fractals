## Question

## Answer
`components/MobileControls.tsx` is at the engine-level `components/` directory, not under `app-gmt/`. Reading the file (`components/MobileControls.tsx:1-169`), the component is entirely camera-agnostic substrate: a `Joystick` primitive (lines 6-113) plus a body that pulls `cameraMode`/`setCameraMode` from `useEngineStore` (line 116) and `isMobile` from `useMobileLayout` (line 117). The only outputs are an Orbit/Fly mode toggle button and two joysticks that emit generic `window` `CustomEvent`s named `joyMove` and `joyLook` (lines 119-125). There is no formula-specific code, no raymarch parameter, no gallery integration, no GMT-app-only store binding — every dependency lives in the engine substrate (`store/engineStore`, `hooks/useMobileLayout`).

Both apps that consume it import from the engine-level path: `app-gmt/AppGmt.tsx:60` (`import MobileControls from '../components/MobileControls'`) and the legacy `App.tsx:2` (`import MobileControls from './components/MobileControls'`). Both gate it the same way (`!isBroadcast(Mode) && <MobileControls />`, AppGmt.tsx:215 / App.tsx:134), confirming it is treated as shared mobile substrate, not as a GMT-app overlay. Combined with q-085's finding that `mobileActiveMenu` is owned by the generic plugins-host (e07), the mobile-controls story belongs to the engine substrate.

Verdict: **keep at `components/MobileControls.tsx` — it IS the engine substrate already.** The surveyor's wording ("GMT-side, not `engine/components/`") reflects a folder-layout misconception: `components/` at the repo root IS the engine UI substrate in dev/'s flat layout, not GMT-app-private. No move needed; the e12 doc should clarify that `components/` here is the shared substrate location, not a GMT-app folder.
