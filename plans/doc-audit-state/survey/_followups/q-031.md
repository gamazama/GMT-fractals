## Question
> `Uniforms.ModularParams`, `LightCount`/`LightType`/etc., `EnvMapTexture`, env CDF samplers, `InterlaceEnabled`/`InterlaceInterval`/`InterlaceStartIter`, `Vec2A`..`Vec4C` (engine/UniformNames.ts:28-92) appear in the names table but not in `BASE_SCHEMA` — they're feature-contributed. Reserved for the relevant feature subsystems to confirm.

## Answer

Two contribution paths are in play (both harvested by `featureRegistry.getUniformDefinitions()` and merged into the schema at `engine-gmt/engine/UniformSchema.ts:77-83`):

1. **`extraUniforms: UniformDefinition[]`** on the `FeatureDefinition` — explicit declarations whose `name`, `type`, `arraySize`, `default`, and `backingOnly` are passed through verbatim. Used for array-typed and texture-typed slots that don't bind to a single React param.
2. **`params.<key>.uniform: string`** descriptor — the pattern from q-030. The schema assembler converts the param's `type` to a GLSL type and uses `default` as the uniform default.

Plus one correction to the question's framing: `EnvRotationMatrix` IS in `BASE_SCHEMA` (`engine-gmt/engine/UniformSchema.ts:73`), so it isn't feature-contributed despite living in the "Environment" section of `UniformNames.ts`.

### Notes on consumption / write-side

- Light-array values are packed by `UniformManager` (`engine-gmt/engine/managers/UniformManager.ts:261-274`) from the light list, then read by the lighting shader chunks (`shaders/chunks/lighting/*.ts`) and the path tracer (`shaders/chunks/pathtracer.ts`).
- Env CDF textures are written by `MaterialController.applyEnvCDF` (`engine-gmt/engine/MaterialController.ts:538-548`); CDF build is owned by `features/reflections/env_cdf.ts` (referenced in the lighting `extraUniforms` comment at `:137-138`).
- `uEnvMapTexture` upload happens through the worker bridge (`engine/worker/renderWorker.ts:553,583`) and the param-driven `setUniform(Uniforms.EnvMapTexture, ...)` in `MaterialController.ts:496`.
- `uModularParams` is `backingOnly: true` — the Three.js uniform exists by default, but the GLSL declaration is added only when a modular formula calls `builder.addUniform(Uniforms.ModularParams, 'float', MAX_MODULAR_PARAMS)` from `core_math.contributeShader` at `:160`. `MaterialController.ts:562-565` mirrors the array into the histogram material.
- Interlace uniforms are also re-declared at the shader-builder boundary (`engine-gmt/engine/SDFShaderBuilder.ts:181-183`, `:709`) and inside the rewriter (`features/interlace/glslRewriter.ts:327-329`). The `params.*.uniform` descriptors in `interlace/index.ts` are the source of truth for the Three.js binding side; the SDFShaderBuilder list is for declaration ordering in generated GLSL.

## Confidence
high — every name traces to an exact line; the assembler call at `UniformSchema.ts:77` is the single funnel; both contribution paths (`extraUniforms` and `params.*.uniform`) are visible in the consulted feature files.

## Related findings

- **Sibling investigation q-030 was incomplete.** It claimed feature-contributed uniforms come exclusively from `params.*.uniform`. In reality `FeatureDefinition.extraUniforms: UniformDefinition[]` is the other (older) path, and the entire lighting subsystem plus the env CDF samplers plus `uModularParams` rely on it. A Phase 2 writer covering UniformSchema or the DDFS feature contract should document both paths. (`engine-gmt/features/lighting/index.ts:124-144`, `engine-gmt/features/core_math.ts:131`.)
- **`backingOnly` flag** — undocumented in the survey but used by `uModularParams` to keep the Three.js uniform live without emitting a GLSL declaration. Declaration is then added on demand by the feature's `contributeShader` via `builder.addUniform`. Worth surfacing in the shader-builder doc. (`engine-gmt/engine/UniformSchema.ts:19-22`, `engine-gmt/features/core_math.ts:131,160`.)
- **`EnvRotationMatrix` is in BASE_SCHEMA, not feature-contributed**, even though it sits visually under the "Environment" section of `UniformNames.ts:53`. The survey's grouping of "env stuff = feature-contributed" misled q-031's framing. (`engine-gmt/engine/UniformSchema.ts:73`.)
- **Interlace declarations are duplicated** between the feature's `params.*.uniform` descriptors (for the Three.js side) and a hardcoded `'uInterlaceEnabled'/'uInterlaceInterval'/'uInterlaceStartIter'` list in `engine-gmt/engine/SDFShaderBuilder.ts:709`. If a Phase 2 writer documents either, they should note the second is a builder-side reserved-name list that pins declaration ordering — both sites need to stay in sync.
- **`uFogColorLinear` is also in BASE_SCHEMA** (`engine-gmt/engine/UniformSchema.ts:74`), not in the original question, but it lives under the "Environment" section header of `UniformNames.ts:59` and could trip up a Phase 2 writer the same way `EnvRotationMatrix` does. Worth flagging that `UniformNames.ts` section headers are organizational comments only and do not correlate with the `BASE_SCHEMA` vs feature-contributed split.
