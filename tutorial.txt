Lesson 3: The Look (Shader & Lighting)
'Shaping light on mathematical surfaces'

-Materials
The Shader tab controls how the surface responds to light. Open the Shader tab.
(trigger - Shader tab open)

-Diffuse & Specular
Diffuse controls overall brightness. Specular controls how shiny the surface is — increase it for a wet or polished look.
Try adjusting both.

-Roughness
Roughness controls how sharp reflections are. 0 is mirror-smooth, 1 is matte like chalk.
Set it below 0.3 to see sharp highlights.

-Metallic
Metallic changes the reflection model. At 0 the surface is plastic or stone. At 1 it reflects its own colour like chrome or gold.
Try setting it to 1.

-Rim Light
Rim Light adds a glow around edges — a Fresnel effect. Increase the intensity and adjust sharpness.

-Emission
Self-Illumination makes the surface glow. Try switching the source to Layer 1 — only the gradient colour will emit light.

-Ambient Occlusion
AO darkens crevices and holes. Increase intensity and spread to see the effect. Try the AO Tint colour to shade shadows.

-Lighting
Open the Light tab. GMT has up to 8 lights in a PBR studio.
(trigger - Light tab open)

-Light Types
Light 1 is the key light. Try dragging the gizmo sphere in the viewport to reposition it.
Toggle between Point and Directional (Sun) to see the difference.

-Colour & Intensity
Change the light colour. Try a warm orange. Increase intensity above 2 for bloom.
Switch to Colour Temperature mode for a Kelvin slider (3000K warm, 6500K daylight, 9000K cool).

-Shadows
Enable shadows on Light 1. Increase softness for area-light penumbra.
If you see black speckles (shadow acne), increase the Bias slightly.

-Attachment Mode
Toggle between Headlamp (moves with camera) and World (fixed in scene). Headlamp is great for exploration, World for cinematic setups.


Lesson 4: Colour & Gradients
'Painting with mathematics'

-Gradient Basics
Open the Gradient tab. Colour in GMT comes from mapping a mathematical value to a gradient.
(trigger - Gradient tab open)

-Mapping Modes
The dropdown at the top selects what value drives the colour. Try switching between Orbit Trap, Iterations, and Radial to see very different looks from the same gradient.

-Editing Knots
Click the gradient bar to add a colour stop. Drag it to reposition. Click a knot to open the colour picker.
Drag a knot off the bar to delete it. Try making a 3-colour gradient.

-Scale & Offset
Scale stretches the pattern (more repetitions). Offset shifts it along the surface. Phase rotates the starting point.
Use the histogram to see the distribution while adjusting.

-Bias Handles
Right-click the gradient to enable bias handles. The diamond between knots shifts the interpolation midpoint — great for fine-tuning transitions.

-Layer 2
Layer 2 adds a second colour layer with its own mapping and gradient. Set a blend mode — try Add for glowing veins or Multiply for grime.

-Procedural Noise (Layer 3)
The noise layer adds 3D simplex noise. Increase Scale for fine grain (metallic), decrease for large blobs. Bump adds surface displacement — essential for rock and rust.

-Image Texturing
Load an image texture for photo-real surfaces. U and V mapping control how the image wraps. Try Decomposition (U) + Iterations (V) for natural flow.


Lesson 5: Scene & Atmosphere
'Setting the stage'

-Scene Panel
Open the Scene tab. This controls camera optics, atmosphere, colour correction, and world transforms.
(trigger - Scene tab open)

-Depth of Field
Camera Blur adds defocus. Set a value above 0, then use Pick Focus to click a surface point. The fractal converges when the camera is still (needs temporal accumulation).

-Fog
Scroll down to Atmosphere. Increase Fog Density and set a Fog Colour. Adjust Start and End distance to control the rolloff. Dark fog creates depth, bright fog creates mist.

-Volumetric Scatter (God Rays)
If enabled in Engine settings, Volumetric Scatter adds light shafts through fog. Adjust Density and Anisotropy — positive g scatters forward (sun shafts), negative g scatters backward.

-Colour Grading
Under Effects, try different Tone Mapping modes: ACES for cinematic warmth, AgX for modern filmic. Adjust Saturation and Levels (black/white points).

-Bloom
Bloom adds glow around bright areas. Works best with high specular or emission. Set a Threshold and increase Intensity.

-Chromatic Aberration
Adds colour fringing at the edges of the frame. Subtle values look photographic, high values are stylised.

-Composition Overlays
From the Camera menu, enable a composition guide: Rule of Thirds, Golden Ratio, or Spiral. Use these to frame your shot before a snapshot or render.


Lesson 6: Animation & Timeline
'Bringing fractals to life'

-Opening the Timeline
Press T to open the Timeline. The toolbar has playback controls, recording, and export.
(trigger - Timeline visible)

-Recording Keyframes
Click the red Record button. Move the playhead to a frame, then change any slider — a keyframe is created automatically. Move the playhead again and change the value. Press Space to play.

-Camera Keyframes
Use the KEY CAM button to capture position, rotation, and zoom as a single keyframe. Record a camera path in Fly mode, then refine in the Graph Editor.

-Graph Editor
Click the curve icon to switch to the Graph Editor. Here you can see and edit animation curves. Drag tangent handles to control easing. Try Smooth and Bounce tools from the toolbar.

-Interpolation
Select keyframes and change interpolation: Bezier (smooth), Linear (constant speed), Step (instant jump). Use Flat tangents for ease-in/ease-out.

-LFO Modulators
Add an LFO from the modulation panel. Choose a waveform (Sine, Triangle, Sawtooth, Noise), set a target parameter, and adjust Period and Strength. LFOs add automatic rhythmic animation without keyframes.

-Video Export
Click the Render button in the timeline toolbar. Choose resolution, format, bitrate, and samples per frame. Higher samples = cleaner frames but slower export. Use Disk Mode on Chrome for large exports.


Lesson 7: Advanced Features
'Power user tools'

-Advanced Mode
Press ` (backtick) to toggle Advanced Mode. This unlocks Engine Settings, compile-time features, and additional controls in every panel.

-Engine Settings
Open Engine Settings from the System Menu. The status dots show which changes need recompilation (yellow = pending). Click Apply to recompile.
Compile-time features include: Raymarched Reflections, Volumetric Scatter, Stochastic Shadows, Water Plane.

-Reflections
Enable Raymarched Reflections in Engine Settings. Set Metallic high and Roughness low in the Shader tab to see mirror-like surfaces. Adjust Max Bounces and Trace Steps for quality vs speed.

-Water Plane
Enable Water Plane in Engine Settings. Adjust Height to position the water level. Wave Strength, Speed, and Frequency control the surface. Works best with Reflections enabled.

-Modular Graph Builder
Switch the formula to Modular. The Graph tab opens a node editor. Add nodes from the dropdown — Transforms, Folds, Fractals, CSG combiners. Wire them together to build custom fractal formulas. Bind node parameters to global sliders (Param A-D) for animation.

-Path Tracing
Switch Render Mode to Path Tracing for physically-accurate global illumination. It's slow — the image converges over time via accumulation. Use for final stills, not exploration. Emission becomes true light in this mode.

-Bucket Rendering
Click the grid icon for high-quality tiled rendering. Set Export Scale (2x = 4K from 1080p). Lower the Convergence Threshold for production quality (0.1%). The viewport locks during render.

-Formula Workshop
Open Formula Workshop from the System Menu. Import .frag files from Fragmentarium or edit GLSL formulas directly. GMT can convert many fragment shaders to native formulas automatically.

-Audio Reactivity
Enable Audio from the System Menu. Choose a source (microphone, system audio, or audio file). Create links from frequency ranges to any parameter. Use Attack/Decay/Smooth to shape the response. Bass for kicks, mids for vocals, treble for hats.

-Hybrid/Box Fold
In the Formula panel, the Hybrid Box section injects geometric folding before the main formula. Try different fold types (Tetra, Octa, Menger) to create fractal-within-fractal structures. Use the Lock icon for advanced interleaved mixing.

-Camera Slots
Press Ctrl+1 through Ctrl+9 to save camera positions. Press the number key alone to recall. Use the Camera Manager panel for named cameras with thumbnails and Quick Views (Front, Top, Isometric, etc).

-Broadcast Mode
Press B to hide all UI for a clean viewport. Press B again or Esc to return. Perfect for screenshots, presentations, or streaming.

-Saving & Sharing
Save your work as .gmf from the System Menu. Share a URL link (copies to clipboard). Snapshots (camera icon) save PNG files with embedded scene data — load any snapshot to restore the full scene.


Lesson 8: Formulas & Exploration
'A gallery of infinite worlds'

-Formula Library
GMT includes 40+ fractal formulas across several categories. Open the formula dropdown to browse the gallery.

-Featured Fractals
Mandelbulb: The classic 3D Mandelbrot extension. Power controls the symmetry fold.
Mandelbox: A box-folding fractal with Scale as the primary control. Scale near -1.5 creates intricate folded structures.
Menger Sponge: A cube with recursive holes. Perfect cube geometry at integer iterations.

-Geometric & Folding
Sierpinski: Triangular recursive subdivision. Icosahedral variants create 20-sided symmetry.
Kaleidoscopic IFS: Infinite reflections with fold operations. Great for crystalline and architectural forms.
Polyhedra: Platonic solids as fractal seeds — Dodecahedron, Icosahedron, Octahedron, and more.

-Hybrids & Experiments
AmazingBox: The Amazing Surface formula with box/sphere folds.
Bristorbrot: A triplex variant creating organic branching.
Pseudo Kleinian: Creates infinite repeating structures and pseudo-Kleinian limit sets.

-Exploration Tips
Every formula responds differently to the same parameters. When you switch formulas, the default preset loads — it's a curated starting point.
Use Julia Mode on any formula to access a hidden dimension of shapes.
Combine formulas with Hybrid Box Fold for unique structures.
Try extreme parameter values — fractals often have interesting behaviour at the boundaries.

-Quick Edit
Hover the viewport and press 1-6 to pop up a slider for Param A-F at your cursor. No need to open the panel.
